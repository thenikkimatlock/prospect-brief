> **Why it costs them:** Customers searching HVAC on Google see the star count before they see anything else. > **Why your offer fits:** Every call the receptionist handles ends with an automated review request text to the customer. Structured review collection is a byproduct of the system, not an add-on.

> **Why your offer fits:** Tangentially relevant: if the phone number were click-to-call AND went to an AI that answered on the first ring 24/7, the mobile conversion problem compounds positively rather than negatively.

**FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE**

Default (no response after first contact):
- **Day 1** (same day as call): Send the email above
- **Day 3**: Short follow-up referencing the mobile phone number hook (see Day 3 hooks below)
- **Day 5**: Phone call attempt — use voicemail script if no answer
- **Day 7**: "Sending the breakdown either way." Link the Loom or PDF audit
- **Day 14**: Move to dormant, 60-day re-check flag

If email opened 2+ times but no reply: skip generic Day 3 hook. Send: "Did this land at a bad time, or is the missed-call angle not the right fit for where you are right now?"

If bounced: switch to phone-only. Call Day 1, call Day 3 (voicemail), final call Day 7.

If they said "send me an email" on the call: email IS Day 1. Day 3 = 2 days after email sent.

If they engaged (said yes, asked for pricing, requested more): skip the sequence. Flag as warm lead. Update GHL tag from `prospect-brief` to `warm-lead` and delete the pending follow-up task.

If "already working with someone": move to dormant. Create a 90-day re-check GHL task.
